Why DeskSmash exists

DeskSmash began with a childhood memory of playing the original Desktop Games / Desktop Destroyer on a computer at my parents’ pharmacy, back when simple offline fun meant a lot.

Years later, I wanted to revisit that experience on my Mac. When I could not find an alternative that felt right, the idea evolved into something broader: playful desktop chaos that feels fast, reversible, and well made.

Rather than simply recreating the original, the goal became to build a modern native Mac experience shaped by that spirit and reimagined for today.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it require Screen Recording permissions?

No. DeskSmash does not require Screen Recording permission. It draws effects on its own transparent overlay and does not capture your screen content.

Does it work on older Intel Macs?

Yes. DeskSmash supports both Apple Silicon and Intel Macs on supported macOS versions.

Does it work in fullscreen or across spaces?

DeskSmash is designed for desktop-wide use and works with standard macOS Spaces and fullscreen behavior.

What tools are included?

DeskSmash currently includes 13 tools across its modes: Hammer, Chainsaw, Machine Gun, Flamethrower, Baseball Bat, Ribbon, Confetti Gun, Bubble Gun, Color Thrower, Phaser, Stamp, Termites, and Polishing Cloth.
